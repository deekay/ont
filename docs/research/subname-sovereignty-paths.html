ONT Root And Subname Sovereignty Paths

Preliminary scale model: roots are the direct Bitcoin-sovereign primitive; subnames are the scalable namespace path, with optional hardening for higher-assurance cases.
Draft framing for discussion
Roots require Bitcoin footprint and maturity, but are the cleanest "just own a name" path.
Subnames can be issued without a new UTXO if the root grants ownership to a subname key.
Finality can come from grant acceptance, checkpointing, and a non-revocation rule.

Key distinction

A finalized subname can be self-sovereign against the root operator: the root cannot revoke or replace the subname owner's key. It is still namespace sovereignty, not identical to owning the root itself.

Resolver role

Resolvers verify roots, grants, owner-key update chains, and proofs. They provide data availability and witness infrastructure; they should not become registrars.
